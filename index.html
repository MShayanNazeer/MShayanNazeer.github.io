Hi!

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at UMass Amherst, advised by Taqi Raza, where I work at the intersection of AI and network systems.

My research asks how cellular and edge infrastructure must evolve to support AI inference that is stateful, resource-intensive, and mobile. I design systems for network-aware LLM serving, including mechanisms for placing, transferring, and reconstructing inference state as users move across edge servers. More broadly, I develop new abstractions and architectures that jointly manage models, compute, memory, and network state to make edge inference efficient, adaptive, and reliable.

Previously, I was a Research Intern at Nokia Bell Labs, where I worked on intelligent mobility optimization for 5G networks and developed an RL-based handover mechanism that resulted in a filed patent. I hold a Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering from NUST, where I graduated with a Gold Medal.
